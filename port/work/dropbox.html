Dropbox, Arduino & Floppy Drive

During my module “Physical Digital Product” here in the University of Dundee, we were asked to design a product to convey sound into meaningful information. I worked in this project along with two coleagues, Jana Mižutytė and Tangui Ness during the second semester of 2013. After some ethnographic research, we decided to design a physical box that responds how much space you have available in your Dropbox account, throught the sound of a floppy-driver. We chose to bring the concept of the old floppy driver, which was the old way to store files, and in this product the main interaction happens when the user insert the floppy disk. For this project, we used Arduino, Processing and the Dropbox API to complete our work. (All the details and design process of this project are here on my blog).
